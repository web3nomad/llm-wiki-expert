Design Philosophy: The Less AI, the More AI

Real Over Synthetic, But With Drama — atypica prefers real human photography, real environments, honest moments. The visual language should feel like it was made by humans, not by AI.

Professional But Not Distant — sophisticated, consultancy-level precision combined with emotional warmth.

Visual Language Spectrum:
- Cinematic: For consumer insights, brand stories, social media impact. Dramatic lighting, strong color contrast, human presence in environmental context.
- Architectural: For strategic analysis, structural thinking, data-driven insights. Geometric composition, monumental scale.
- Documentary: For authentic consumer moments, field research, human stories. Natural light, real environments, honest moments.
- Editorial: For reports, presentations, professional documents. Sophisticated typography, restrained color.

What atypica avoids: Neon gradients. Drop shadows and glows. Multiple competing colors. Infographic-style layouts. Stock photo composites. 3D rendered objects floating in space.

Test standards for visuals:
- Does it make you stop when scrolling quickly?
- Does it hold up alongside top brands/publications?
- Does it stand out among AI product images as different?
- Without text, does the visual itself convey the right emotion?
- At 100x100px, are core elements still clear?

Core insight: The product simulates human consumers — so the brand itself must feel deeply human. The less it looks like AI, the more powerfully AI it is.
